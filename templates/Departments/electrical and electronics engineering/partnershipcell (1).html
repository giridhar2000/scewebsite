Formation of Institute Industry Partnership Cell - EEE

The department of EEE took a initiative on knowledge sharing by providing consultancy services to the industry by leveraging the expertise of the faculty and the existing laboratory facilities of the department. As a result of continuous effort of the department, the IIPC-EEE cell was formulated on October 2012 with an objective to reduce the gap between industry expectations (practice) and academic offerings (theory) by direct involvement of industry to attain a symbiosis. The IIPC-EEE is headed by a Certified Energy Auditor.

The department of EEE has procured a Fluke power quality and energy analyzer worth Rs. 8,00,000 (by utilizing the Grants sanctioned by AICTE (MHRD) under MODROBS scheme to carry out power quality and energy audit in industries. The area of power quality and energy audit is identified as a potential area with good scope for extending consultancy services especially due to the recent TNEB regulations (April 2012) to implement the “Central Electricity Authority (Technical Standards for Connectivity to the Grid) Regulations, 2007”, which has a specific clause on power quality compliance.

Objectives of the Cell

It is observed that the benefit of the modern technology has not reached most of the small scale industries and startup companies, where still age old engineering practices are being followed leading to power quality issues and energy loss. It is also found that the awareness level on power quality and energy conservation were not up to the mark in a certain sector of small scale industries.

One among the main objective of the IIPC - EEE is to cater these sectors of the society, to derive the real benefit of technology at the grass root level industries and thereby contribute to energy conservation and power quality improvement in line with the nation 's energy policy.

Thrust area in which consultancy is being offered

The department is currently offering the consultancy services (but not limited to) in the following areas leveraging the facilities of the department and the expertise of faculty in this field.

  • Power Quality Compliance Audit for HT customers
  • Energy Audit for industries
  • Renewable energy

Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with GRK Electronics, Chennai and Agro-Tech, Dindigul

The department is also working on to provide a solution wherever there is lack of power quality compliance as per norms. To carry out further development work in this front, the department has entered into an MOU with industry partners working in this area.

Two MOUs were signed by EEE department with two industries GRK Electronics, Chennai and Agro-Tech, Dindigul to promote consultancy services.

  1. MOU signed with GRK electronic, Chennai for development and implementation of power quality mitigation techniques with a financial commitment of INR 300000 /- from the industry partner
  2. MOU signed with Agro-Tech Dindigul for development of efficient solar dryer with an financial commitment of INR 500000/- from the industry partner

Other Activities

Two Days Development Workshop on PCB routing Design and Fabrication - 2013, 2014 and 2015

About the Workshop

The IIPC-EEE (Institute –Industry partnership Cell) is planning to conduct a two-day skills development workshop on PCB designing and fabrication for III EEE students and I PG students, which is an all in one complete module, on converting a white paper schematic to working PCB. This is expected to help the students to implement their academic project work by self, rather than outsourcing. The workshop is scheduled on 21 & 22/02/2014 and 7-12-2015 TO 9-12-2015.

A first of its kind knowledge sharing initiative, two-day skills development workshop in our college, on PCB designing and fabrication, an all in one complete module, on converting a white paper schematic to functionally working PCB, organized by IIPC-EEE (Institute-Industry Partnership Cell), Department of EEE with the following objectives

  • Knowledge sharing within the department
  • Impart the necessary skills to the participants, that is required to convert a white paper schematic to a working PCB
  • Share the knowledge on PCB designing learnt by final year PG students over a period of time
  • Transfer of knowledge skills on PCB designing to the next batch of students
  • Achieve self reliance in designing and fabricating a PCB for a given circuit by all participants

Best PCB Contest

Encouraging prizes will be awarded to the participants for the best PCB design and fabrication, by the industry partner GRK electronics

Expected Outcome of the Workshop

  • All the participants becoming self reliant and confident in designing and fabricating a PCB by their own for a given circuit
  • Leverage the skills on PCB designing in implementing and realizing some basic analog controllers used for drives that could enhance the understanding level on subject in current semester and to come out with a mini project.
  • Utilize the skills for academic project work (phase-1 and 2) to achieve indigenous implementation without a need for external support
  • Achieve self reliance in designing and fabricating a PCB for a given circuit by all student participants

Target participants

First year PG students shall participate in this workshop. A separate workshop could be planned for final year UG students after this, as the numbers of participants need to be limited as it is a skills development workshop which requires individual attention of each participant with dedicated resource men as well as materials.

Course Fees

There will be a nominal course fees which is collected in order to get a firm commitment from the participants apart from meeting the part of expenses on consumable/tools specifically required for each students participating in the workshop. Based on the above, the subsidized fee is fixed as Rs. 450 per student.
